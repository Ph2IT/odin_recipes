DAL

DESCRIPTION

Dal originated in India to mean lentils. It can be cooked in a few different ways, depending on the region. It is mainly made as a soup with tomatoes and onions, and various spices included, however, in south India, this dish is cooked plain and eaten with rice as the start of a meal.

Dal is never eaten by itself, though it can be, and always paired with chapatis or rotis, or just rice. In south India, dal is also used to make another dish, sambar, which is also a soup but eaten with rice or other Indian delicacies.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup washed and rinsed dal
  • 1 tomato
  • 1 onion
  • ginger
  • cilantro, if desired
  • 2-3 cups of water
  • 2 Tbsp oil
  • mustard seeds
  • cumin seeds
  • turmeric
  • red chili powder
  • salt

STEPS

  1. pressure cook dal with 2 cups of water
  2. in a separate pan, heat oil and add mustard seeds until spluttering
  3. add cumin seeds and ginger
  4. add onions and saut√©
  5. add tomatoes
  6. add salt, turmeric, and red chili powder
  7. cook until tomatoes are cooked
  8. add pressure cooked dal
  9. add water per desired consistency
  10. top off with chopped cilantro, if desired
